What is the Problem?

Through the years, the world saw education as the primary source for the better future of the generations. Children who have skills with reading, writing, and counting are likely to have a better future than those who not. We aim to let the counting generations be the legacy of what we have developed, yet it seems that we still fail to fully give children their well-deserved quality education.

Seeing this, we actually have made significant progress, but usually resulting in one factor sacrificed for another. More schools built, but does quality education still exist? More updates to the curriculum, but can the staff keep up with these? While we have the intent to improve, we have made ourselves a slave of our own wants, and this is the time where unity is a need, not a want. In order to achieve what we envision for the better future, giving SDG 14 a chance is the least we can do.

What is being done?

Governments and organizations are working towards a life of Quality Education. UNESCO, UNICEF, the World Bank, and other organizations have been investing in several projects that will help eventually pull out the potential of all children in the world. Teacher training, education access expansion, modern integration, and most importantly, dealing with inequalities are few of all the goals that SDG 4 wants to attain. With this, the most prioritized is the reduction of out-of-school youth, both globally and locally. By 2030, we hope to see a world with improved comprehension among students and an open space, regardless of race, age, and sexuality, with everyone being able to feel safe and encouraged with learning.

So why am I here?

Even as a student, I witness the harsh reality of life, especially for those who are unprivileged to have the access to right education. While I may not have the authority nor power to command and govern laws for our education system in the Philippines, I still aim to let people know about the importance of education in our lives. While it may be true that some are still able to thrive without it, it still remains clear that education not only gives us the opportunity to learn more, but to also see what's behind the life that is waiting for the generations to come. Education gives us a chance to try and become better versions of ourselves, and eventually leads to us each having a legacy for the world waiting for what we may do.
